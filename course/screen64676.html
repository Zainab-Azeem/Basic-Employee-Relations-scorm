What is a Collective Bargaining Agreement?

A collective bargaining agreement (CBA) may also be called a labor contract or labor agreement. This is a document that establishes the framework for relations between management and the union and contains in writing those working conditions mutually agreed to by union and management during negotiations. A CBA is a legal document, binding on the agency.  The Statute defines a collective bargaining agreement at ยง7103(a)(8)  as, "an agreement entered into as a result of collective bargaining, pursuant to the provisions of this chapter." There is great variety in the conditions of employment that are established by a CBA, though all CBAs are required to contain a negotiated grievance procedure that provides for binding arbitration. 5 U.S.C. 7121
