Bargaining Units Exclusions (continued)

Supervisors and management officials are normally excluded from collective bargaining units, though there may be occasional exceptions because some units of managers and supervisors were grandfathered when the Statute was passed. There are definitions of supervisor, management official, confidential employee, and professional employees contained in Section 7103 of the Statute. 

The FLRA has always narrowly construed the exceptions to being included in bargaining units. So, for example, what we sometimes think of as "confidential" employees may not be what the FLRA has defined in its case law as what is meant by the confidential employee exclusion in the Statute. For purposes of the Statute, "Confidential" employees are employees who act in a confidential capacity with respect to an agency management official that formulates or effectuates management policies in the field of Labor-Management Relations. An example of a confidential employee in this instance would be the Executive Assistant to the Chief of Labor Relations for an agency.

We will further explore definitions for supervisors and management officials in this module.
