Step 2: Provide an Opportunity to Improve

Providing an Opportunity to Improve

Before you help the supervisor start the formal process of taking a performance-based action, be aware that the supervisor has options. The law provides for two different processes for taking performance-based actions. If a performance-based action is taken under title 5 CFR part 432, a formal opportunity to improve is required. If a performance-based action is taken under title 5 CFR part 752, an opportunity period is not required. Step Two will walk you through the part 432 process of giving an employee a formal opportunity to improve his or her performance. Step Three will provide more details on deciding under which process to take your action. Regardless of the process you use, an opportunity period is a useful tool for assisting employees in improving their performance.
