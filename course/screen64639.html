Local Security, Crisis Management & Evacuation Plans

Each office must develop:

  • Local Security Plan: Identify threats and mitigation measures.
  • Crisis Management Plan: Procedures for emergencies.
  • Evacuation Plan: Safe exit routes, assembly points, and communication channels.

Minimum Training Requirements

Staff must receive training to ensure preparedness:

  • Introduction to safety and security aligned with NIDA-Pak policies.
  • Regional or local training for specific risks.
  • Project managers decide training measures and frequency.

No Ransom or Bribes

NIDA-Pakistan maintains ethical standards:

  • No payment of ransoms for staff release.
  • No rewards, inducements, or bribes to officials.
  • All actions comply with law and organizational ethics.

Right to Withdraw

Staff may withdraw from unsafe situations without disciplinary action:

  • Notify the security office via phone or email.
  • Follow guidance from security personnel.
  • Personal safety is the top priority.

No Right to Remain

NIDA-Pakistan can withdraw staff from dangerous situations:

  • Staff must comply immediately.
  • Failure to comply may result in disciplinary action or contract termination.
  • Applies at project and country program level.
