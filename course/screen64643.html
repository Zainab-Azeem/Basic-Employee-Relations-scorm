Summary - NIDA-Pakistan Security Policy

The NIDA-Pakistan Security Policy ensures that all staff operate safely and ethically in potentially high-risk environments.

Key Points:

  • Impartiality: Staff must remain neutral and not take sides in any conflict.
  • Primacy to Life: Staff safety takes precedence over organizational property or assets.
  • Proportionate Risk: Risks must be weighed against the benefits of the activity.
  • Management Responsibility: Management ensures staffing, training, and policies are in place for safety.
  • Safety and Security: Staff are responsible for personal safety and adherence to security measures.
  • Local Security, Crisis & Evacuation Plans: Each office develops emergency and evacuation procedures.
  • Training: Staff receive introductory and ongoing safety and security training.
  • No Ransom or Bribes: Ethical standards prohibit ransom payments or inducements to officials.
  • Right to Withdraw: Staff may withdraw from unsafe situations without disciplinary action.
  • No Right to Remain: Management may withdraw staff from dangerous situations; compliance is mandatory.

All staff must read, understand, and sign this policy as part of their employment contract. Adherence ensures staff safety, operational effectiveness, and compliance with legal and ethical standards.
