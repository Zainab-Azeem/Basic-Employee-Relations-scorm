23–26. Personnel File, Access, Internship & Employee Benefits

The HR Section will maintain an updated record of service for all staff. Separate files shall be maintained for each staff containing the following information:

  • a) Name
  • b) Father’s Name
  • c) Date of Birth
  • d) Place of Birth (District of permanent residence with the name of the Province)
  • e) Qualification
  • f) Present Address
  • g) Date of Appointment
  • h) Date of Joining Service
  • i) Promotions - dates and grades
  • j) Number of dependents and their relationship to employee
  • k) Copy of Job Description
  • l) Copy of National Identity Card
  • m) Employment History (Experience Details)
  • n) Emergency Contact Person with Phone No and address (if any)
  • o) Legal Heir, for settlement of claims in case of Employee's death
  • p) Documentation of performance evaluations
  • q) Driving license copy is also to be kept, where required

(Specimen attached)

Personnel files are the property of the NIDA-Pak, and access to the information they contain is restricted. An employee can access the personnel file of others or his own only with the approval of the Chief Executive Officer. No other employees are allowed to review personnel files.

The individual responsible to maintain personnel files is responsible to ensure confidentiality and safe keeping of personnel files with restricted and authorized access.

  • i. A copy of the PER will be placed in the employee's personal file.
  • ii. Employees shall study and sign their PER so as to be informed of their perceived strengths and weaknesses, in order to explain their position and improve their behavior.

NIDA-Pak offers internship positions to eligible, presentable and responsible looking candidates from well-known educational institutions who fulfill the eligibility criteria. Only a small number of candidates may be offered internships at a given point in time, as per guidelines issued by HR Section.

  • 25.1.1. Duration of Internship: Up to maximum of six months.
  • 25.1.2. Eligibility: Minimum educational qualification shall be issued by concerned Division/Unit of the NIDA-Pak.
  • 25.1.3. Monthly Stipend: NIDA-Pak believes in individual capability and acknowledges the services provided by employees or interns. NIDA-Pak also encourages volunteerism and accepts interns (both paid and unpaid).
  • 25.1.4. Approval Authority: The HR Section recommends applicants for internship subject to final approval of Chief Executive Officer.
  • 25.1.5. Selection Procedure:
    • a) Applications and CVs may be received in the Head Office and Regional Office(s).
    • b) The initiating office will conduct selection interviews to determine eligibility.
    • c) Selected interns must provide CVs, a photograph, copy of CNIC, copies of testimonials, and a letter of recommendation from their educational institution.
    • d) HR Section will issue an “internship letter” which will be accepted by interns under their signatures.
    • e) On completion of internship, supervising officers will complete the Internship Evaluation Form, which will be returned to HR Section for issuance of certificates and future reference.

Internship Certificate is an official document and may only be issued by the HR Section with approval of the Chief Executive Officer and after successful completion of Internship period. No other branch/unit or office of the NIDA-Pak is authorized to issue Internship Certificate.

NIDA-Pak employees are entitled to a variety of benefits as per organizational policies, covering different categories of leave and other entitlements.

Leave is earned by duty only and cannot be claimed as a matter of right. It may be granted, refused, or revoked by competent authority according to the exigencies of service.

No leave may be granted until a report on admissibility has been obtained from the records section. Admissible leave may then be sanctioned by the competent authority.

  • 24 working days per calendar year (2 days credited monthly).
  • Not more than 18 working days at a time.
  • Un-availed leave lapses at year-end (December 31).
  • Accumulated leave allowed up to 6 months. Excess credited only for serious illness (e.g., TB, Cancer, AIDS).
  • Up to 12 working days per year (1 day credited monthly).
  • Only for urgent reasons.
  • Maximum of 3 working days at a time.
  • Cannot be combined with other leave types.
  • Lapses at year-end, non-en-cashable.
  • 12 working days with pay per year.
  • Medical Certificate required if exceeding 3 days.
  • Not en-cashable under any circumstances.

Up to 12 weeks with pay (4 weeks before and 8 weeks after delivery). Applicable only after 1 year of service. Maximum 2 confinements with 2 years gap. Birth must be notified within 7 days.

Up to 5 days with pay (2 days before and 3 days after delivery). Applicable only after 1 year of service. Maximum 2 confinements with 2 years gap. Birth must be notified within 7 days.

Maximum 10 working days with supervisor recommendation and CEO approval.

1–3 days at a time for work on holidays/weekends. Must be requested immediately after duty or within 1 month. Maximum 12 days per year, with supervisor approval. Can be combined with earned or casual leave.

Up to 365 working days without pay for exceptional reasons (Hajj/Umrah, study abroad, overseas visit) with CEO approval. Written request required.

Holidays as declared by Government of Pakistan. Administration will issue a yearly list from the official Gazette. Applicable to all employees immediately upon employment.

  • Casual Leave: At least 24 hours in advance
  • Earned Leave: At least 7 days in advance
  • Medical Leave: On occurrence
  • Other Leaves: On occurrence or at least 24 hours in advance
