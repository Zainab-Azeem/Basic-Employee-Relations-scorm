Procurement Documentation Process

The procurement process requires a systematic approach to ensure transparency, accountability, value for money, and compliance with organizational and donor regulations. The following documents constitute the essential steps and records in the procurement cycle, from initial demand identification to final acknowledgment of goods or services received.

  1. Requisition Form: Formal request by department.
  2. Call for Quotations: Invitation to suppliers.
  3. Valid Quotations: Signed and stamped quotations.
  4. Comparative Statements: Evaluation of quotations.
  5. Purchase Order: Legal purchase confirmation.
  6. Goods Received Notes (GRN): Confirmation of receipt.
  7. Original Invoices: Proof for payment.
  8. Cross Cheque (Photo Copy): Audit record.
  9. Taxation: Compliance documents.
  10. Acknowledgment on Letterhead: Supplier confirmation.
