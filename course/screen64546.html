Procurement Documentation Proces

The procurement process requires a systematic approach to ensure transparency, accountability, value for money, and compliance with organizational and donor regulations. The following documents constitute the essential steps and records in the procurement cycle, from initial demand identification to final acknowledgment of goods or services received.

  1. Requisition Form: A formal request initiated by the requesting department detailing the required goods or services, specifications, estimated cost, and justification for procurement. It serves as the basis for initiating the procurement process.
  2. Call for Quotations: An official invitation sent to vendors or suppliers to submit price quotations. This step promotes competition, transparency, and ensures that the organization receives competitive market rates.
  3. Valid Quotations: Received quotations from suppliers, duly signed and stamped, meeting the required specifications, validity period, and compliance with procurement standards.
  4. Comparative Statements: A documented analysis comparing all valid quotations based on price, quality, delivery time, warranty, and other relevant criteria. This helps in selecting the most suitable vendor.
  5. Purchase Order: A legally binding document issued to the selected vendor confirming the purchase details, including quantities, agreed price, delivery terms, and payment conditions.
  6. Goods Received Notes (GRN): A record confirming that the ordered goods or services have been received in the correct quantity and quality, verified by the receiving department.
  7. Original Invoices: Supplier invoices that provide proof of delivery and financial documentation for processing payments, ensuring alignment with the purchase order and GRN.
  8. Cross Cheque (Photo Copy): A photocopy of the issued cross cheque used for supplier payment, serving as a financial record for audit and transparency purposes.
  9. Taxation: Relevant withholding tax deductions, tax exemption certificates, or tax documentation, maintained to comply with statutory and donor requirements.
  10. Acknowledgment on Letterhead: A signed and stamped acknowledgment from the supplier on their official letterhead, confirming receipt of payment and successful completion of delivery.
