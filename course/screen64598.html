Step 1: Communicate Expectations and Performance Problems

Providing Positive and Constructive Feedback

Being in a working environment, we need to apply and respond to feedback. We receive feedback all the time from the tone of people’s voices, their body language, and the way people interact with us. This kind of feedback is needed all the time to maintain open communications with our employees in order to get the job done more effectively. Performance feedback can be given through constructive and positive feedback.

Constructive feedback is communication that brings to an individual’s attention an area in which their performance could improve, in a manner that helps the individual understand and internalize the information. Constructive feedback does not focus on fault or blame; it is specific and is directed towards the action, not the person.
