Past Practices (continued)

What happens if a past practice is inconsistent with the CBA? Generally, where parties have included a matter in their negotiated agreement, the agreement will govern unit employees’ conditions of employment. Occasionally, parties can establish conditions of employment that are inconsistent with the terms of the CBA. Recent FLRA case law has clarified that it is possible to modify express contract language contained in a CBA by past practice. 66 FLRA 963 is the leading case on modifying a CBA provision by past practice. According to the FLRA, when this occurs it is said to be a matter of contract interpretation for the arbitrator or administrative law judge who is determining the parties’ intent with respect to the meaning of the provision by looking to the practice of the parties in applying the provision. Supervisors should be advised that if the union makes a past practice claim, they need to immediately consult with their assigned Labor Relations Specialist.
