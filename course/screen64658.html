Definition of Supervisor
  • The definition of "supervisor" may be found at 5 U.S.C. 7103(a)(10):
    • A person with authority, in the interest of the agency, to take, or to effectively recommend at least one of the following actions, with respect to at least one Federal employee, if the exercise is not merely routine or clerical in nature, but requires the consistent exercise of independent judgment:
      • Hire, promote, recall, discipline
      • Adjust grievances, assign, reward, lay off
      • Remove, direct, transfer
      • Suspend, furlough.

For bargaining units that include firefighters or nurses, a slightly different definition of “supervisor” is applied. There, the term “supervisor” will include only those individuals who devote a preponderance of their employment time to exercising such authority. 

The definition of supervisor, and how it will be applied in specific factual situations has been clarified by FLRA case law. See 3 FLRA 596, 35 FLRA 1206 which may be found on www.flra.gov. 

The key thing to note is that the FLRA will use this definition to determine which employees must be excluded from any bargaining unit as a supervisor, and which employee may be included in the bargaining unit. The FLRA’s application of this definition may differ from the agency’s own classification of positions as “supervisory.”
