Indications of Formal Discussions

The following are indications of formal discussions:

  • The level of the management official
  • Whether more than one management official attended
  • Where the meeting took place
  • How long the meeting lasted
  • How was the meeting called
  • Was there a formal agenda
  • Whether employee attendance was mandatory
  • The manner in which the meeting was conducted

The word "formal" was not defined in the Federal Service Labor-Management Relations Statute. These indications of formality are taken from FLRA case law. Their case law also provides examples of meetings or discussions that were found not meet this standard of formality. For instance, when an employee initiated the meeting and the other factors of formality were not present.
