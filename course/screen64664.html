The Negotiation Process

As mentioned on a previous screen, the Statute requires both agencies and unions to bargain in good faith. This includes an agency providing a union with advance notice and an opportunity to bargain prior to making changes to conditions of employment. The Statute also has provisions the parties must follow to resolve disputes that may arise during the negotiation process. For example, disputes over whether the parties have an obligation to bargain may be addressed through the unfair labor practice process (discussed on a later screen). Disputes over whether bargaining proposals are “negotiable,” meaning that the parties must engage in a give-and-take over their substance, are addressed through negotiability appeals. If the agency and union have engaged in good faith bargaining over negotiable proposals, but are unable to reach agreement, they may be at impasse. The Statute provides for resolution of impasses through assistance by the Federal Mediation and Conciliation Service (FMCS) or resolution by the Federal Service Impasses Panel (FSIP), as strikes and lockouts are prohibited for Federal employees. Generally, the agency may not implement changes to conditions of employment until the bargaining process is complete.
