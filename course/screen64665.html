Conditions of Employment

The term "conditions of employment" is defined in the Statute at 5 U.S.C. 7103(a)(14). Conditions of employment refer to personnel policies, practices, and matters, whether established by rule, regulation or otherwise, affecting working condition

The statutory definition of "conditions of employment" is extremely broad. Examples include: telework policies, call-in procedures, promotion policies, and discipline procedures.  These examples are just a few that would come under the subject matter of "conditions of employment." Note, also, that "working conditions" is synonymous with "conditions of employment."

The FLRA has a two-part test for determining if a matter involves a condition of employment of employees in the bargaining unit: 1) whether the proposal pertains to bargaining unit employees; and 2) whether there is a nexus or connection between the proposal and the work situation or employment relationship of bargaining unit employees.
