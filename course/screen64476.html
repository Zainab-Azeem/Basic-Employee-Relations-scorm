5. Adherence to Security Policies

All employees, volunteers, partners, and contributors of NIDA-Pakistan are required to fully comply with the organisation’s local and organisational security policies applicable to their place of work. This includes adhering to all safety protocols, risk mitigation measures, and contingency procedures established to protect individuals and assets. Under no circumstances should any individual take unnecessary risks that could endanger their own health, safety, or security, or that of fellow team members, project participants, community members, or other stakeholders. Every member of NIDA-Pakistan’s workforce is responsible for exercising sound judgment and prioritising safety in all activities, whether in the field, in the office, or during travel on behalf of the organisation. These security obligations apply not only during working hours but also in situations where a clear link exists between the individual’s actions and NIDA-Pakistan’s representation or operations. Failure to comply with these protocols may result in disciplinary action.

6. No Supporting of Terrorism or Money Laundering

NIDA-Pakistan strictly prohibits the use of any organisational resources—financial, material, or otherwise—for the purpose of money laundering or supporting terrorist activities, whether directly or indirectly. This commitment applies under all circumstances and in all operational contexts, without exception.

All employees, partners, volunteers, and contributors are required to comply fully with the organisation’s Anti-Terrorism Policy and all relevant national and international legal frameworks. It is the personal responsibility of each individual to ensure that NIDA-Pakistan’s activities and resources are never misused in ways that could facilitate terrorism or other unlawful financial activities.

Commitments under this Policy

  • Prohibit the direct or indirect use of resources for terrorism or money laundering.
  • Comply with all provisions of the Anti-Terrorism Policy at all times.
  • Report any suspected violations immediately through designated reporting channels.
