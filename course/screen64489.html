Reporting Obligations & Consequences for Breaches

Anyone who becomes aware of, suspects, or has concerns about a possible breach of the NIDA-Pakistan Code of Conduct or any related policy is required to report the matter promptly to the Compliance Unit. Reports can be submitted by email to crm@nidapakistan.org or via the online reporting form: Submit a Report (online form).

Reports made to line management or through national complaint channels must be forwarded to the Compliance Unit without delay. NIDA-Pakistan also provides a confidential and anonymous reporting option through the online form and a dedicated whistle-blower hotline.

Confidentiality & Protection

  • All reports are treated as strictly confidential in accordance with the Organisational Directive on Whistleblowing.
  • Individuals who report in good faith will not face retaliation or adverse consequences, even if an allegation is later found to be unsubstantiated.
  • Knowingly submitting false allegations to harm others is prohibited and may lead to disciplinary action.

Confirmed breaches of the Code of Conduct or associated policies may result in disciplinary measures, including but not limited to suspension, immediate termination of employment or contracts, and legal action. Where appropriate, NIDA-Pakistan will report criminal offences to the relevant authorities in compliance with applicable laws.
