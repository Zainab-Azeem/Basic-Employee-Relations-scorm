8. Child Protection

NIDA-Pakistan recognises that children require special care, protection, and safeguarding. The safety, dignity, and well-being of all children are a paramount priority in every project, programme, and activity undertaken by the organisation. Any behaviour that could harm, exploit, or endanger children is strictly prohibited.

All employees, volunteers, partners, and contributors must condemn every form of child abuse, including physical, emotional, and sexual abuse, neglect, and exploitation. They are required to act immediately to prevent harm, report suspected cases, and promote environments where children are safe, respected, and empowered.

Commitments under this Policy

  • Uphold the rights of all children in accordance with international conventions and national laws.
  • Actively prevent, identify, and respond to any risk or incident of harm involving children.
  • Never engage in behaviour that could be interpreted as abusive, exploitative, or inappropriate toward children.
  • Advocate for child protection and integrate safeguarding measures in all programmes and activities.
