3.No Discrimination

NIDA-Pakistan maintains a zero-tolerance policy towards any form of Discrimination:1 by its employees, volunteers, partners, or contributors. Discrimination on the basis of age, physical or mental disability, nationality, ethnicity, gender, political opinion, union activity, religion, culture, language, sexual orientation, or any other distinguishing characteristic is strictly prohibited in all organisational activities and interactions. In addition to discrimination, all forms of harassment, mobbing2, and bullying3—whether physical, verbal, psychological, or digital—are unacceptable. This includes the use of inappropriate, offensive, violent, or insulting language in any form, whether spoken, written, or conveyed through images or gestures. These standards apply equally to online behaviour, including the use of social media, emails, and other digital communication platforms. Any form of disrespectful, harmful, or discriminatory conduct—whether directed at colleagues, community members, partners, or any other stakeholder—undermines the values of NIDA-Pakistan and will be addressed with appropriate disciplinary action. NIDA-Pakistan expects all members of its workforce and network to actively promote mutual respect, dignity, and inclusiveness, ensuring that the organisation remains a safe and welcoming environment for everyone.

4. Avoiding Conflicts of Interest

NIDA-Pakistan is committed to ensuring that all organisational decisions and activities are conducted with integrity, impartiality, and in the best interest of the organisation’s mission. Employees, volunteers, partners, and contributors must not exploit their professional role, decision-making authority, or access to organisational resources for personal benefit or to advantage relatives, friends, or associated entities.

All individuals must avoid situations where personal, financial, or other interests could conflict—whether directly or indirectly—with the interests of NIDA-Pakistan. If such situations arise, individuals are required to disclose them immediately and without solicitation to their supervisor or the designated compliance authority. Proactive disclosure is essential to maintaining organisational trust and public confidence.

Key Principles

  • Do not use your organisational position for personal or third-party gain.
  • Avoid decision-making where a personal interest exists.
  • Disclose all potential conflicts of interest immediately and transparently.
