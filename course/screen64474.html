2. No Religious or Political Activities While Representing NIDA-Pakistan

NIDA-Pakistan is committed to maintaining its status as a politically neutral and non-denominational organisation, ensuring that its humanitarian and development work remains impartial, inclusive, and accessible to all communities without bias or discrimination.

To safeguard this neutrality, all employees, volunteers, partners, and contributors must refrain from engaging in any political campaigning, advocacy for specific political parties, or promotion of religious beliefs while performing their duties or representing NIDA-Pakistan in any official capacity. This includes participation in rallies, speeches, religious preaching, public endorsements, or the distribution of political or religious materials under the organisation’s name.

Any form of religious or political involvement conducted as part of official duties is strictly prohibited unless explicit prior approval is granted in writing by NIDA-Pakistan’s Executive Management. Such exceptions will only be considered if the activity is directly aligned with the organisation’s mandate and does not compromise its neutrality.

This policy does not infringe upon the individual’s right to personal freedom of belief and association. Employees and contributors are free to participate in religious or political activities in their private capacity. However, they must ensure that such participation is clearly understood by others as personal, independent, and unrelated to NIDA-Pakistan’s operations. This includes avoiding the use of organisational logos, branding, or affiliations in any personal political or religious context.

By adhering to this policy, NIDA-Pakistan reaffirms its commitment to impartiality, ensuring that the trust of communities, partners, and donors is preserved and that its services remain equally accessible to all, regardless of faith, political views, or affiliations.
