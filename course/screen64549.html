Cash-for-Work Documentation Requirements

The Cash-for-Work (CfW) mechanism requires systematic documentation to ensure transparency, accountability, fair beneficiary selection, and accurate financial tracking. The following documents play a vital role in validating beneficiary participation, confirming payment disbursements, and reconciling financial records.

  • Beneficiaries List: A verified and approved list of eligible participants selected through transparent criteria, including names, identification details, assigned tasks, and duration of engagement under the Cash-for-Work activity.
  • Tokens: Unique identification or payment tokens issued to each beneficiary to authenticate their entitlement for participation and payment, serving as a control mechanism to prevent duplication or fraud.
  • Thumbs/Signs on Receiving: Beneficiary thumb impressions or signatures obtained at the time of payment disbursement as legal proof of receipt, ensuring transparency and accountability.
  • Copies of Cheques: Photocopies of cheques issued for payment to beneficiaries or through financial service providers, serving as documentation for audit, traceability, and financial compliance.
  • Reconciliation: A detailed financial reconciliation sheet comparing approved payments, disbursed amounts, remaining balances, and supporting documents, ensuring accuracy, compliance, and closure of accounts.
