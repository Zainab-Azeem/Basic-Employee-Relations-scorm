Role of the Supervisor in Labor Relations (continued)

In a similar vein, the importance of developing a productive relationship with the Union representatives with which the supervisor and ER/ELR specialists interacts cannot be overstated. A relationship built on mutual respect, understanding, and the active willingness of both Agency management and union representatives to fairly resolve issues and grievances at the lowest organizational level possible will enable supervisors to spend more time focused on mission accomplishment. In order to build these healthy and productive relationships with both the LR specialists and the Union representatives with which they interact, Federal supervisors should be assisted to develop a firm grounding in the law that governs labor relations activity.
