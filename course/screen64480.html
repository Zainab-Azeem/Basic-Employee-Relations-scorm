9. Responsible Handling of Data and Information

NIDA-Pakistan is committed to the lawful, ethical, and secure collection, use, and processing of all information, data, and photographs in line with the principle of data minimisation. Such activities are undertaken only when necessary for fulfilling the organisation’s mission and in full compliance with applicable national and international data protection laws.

All employees, partners, volunteers, and contributors must handle personal and confidential data with the utmost care, ensuring maximum protection against unauthorised access. When collecting data—especially from project participants—individuals must be informed of their data protection rights, including how their information will be used, stored, and safeguarded.

Personal or confidential information may not be shared outside the organisation, whether verbally, in writing, or electronically, without prior written authorisation from NIDA-Pakistan, except where legal obligations require disclosure. In such cases, disclosure must strictly follow the applicable legal framework. Individuals have the right to request information on the personal data stored about them at any time, and such data must be deleted promptly if it is no longer needed, in accordance with the right to be forgotten.

Commitments under this Policy

  • Collect and process only the data essential for organisational work.
  • Protect data from unauthorised access at all stages.
  • Inform individuals of their data rights before collection.
  • Avoid sharing personal or confidential data without written consent.
  • Delete outdated or unnecessary data promptly.
