22. Resignation and Termination of Service

Employees desirous of leaving the service of NIDA-Pak are required to give one month's previous notice in writing, failing which they shall be liable to pay a sum equal to one month's pay.

In case of resignation from job, for the contract or temporary employee, the resignation notice period may vary depending on the employment or project duration. An employee has to inform 15 days prior to the HR department of NIDA-Pak if the project (original or remaining)/employment duration is minimum 3 months and 3–7 days in case the project/employment duration is minimum 10 days, otherwise an employee will be liable to pay sum equal to the resignation period mentioned in the employment contract/agreement.

An employee who tenders his resignation, or gives notice of resignation, while on leave will be treated to have left the service of NIDA-Pak without notice on the day he proceeded on leave.

The resigning employee will submit all the required documents, report and will complete the task assigned before leaving NIDA-Pak. The employee will clear all accounts with NIDA-Pak, and also submit a clearance certificate, stating that he does not hold any official paper, equipment, or any property of the NIDA-Pak, and has no dues outstanding against him, which shall be duly verified by officers concerned on the prescribed form before being relieved by NIDA-Pak.

Services of an employee may be terminated any time on grounds of indiscipline, financial imbalance, gross misconduct, and gross negligence, without prior notice. Executive Director is the ONLY authority to terminate an employee. Staff terminated on the above grounds shall not be entitled to severance pay or other benefits normally admissible to staff leaving the service of NIDA-Pak.

The services of an employee may be terminated by giving one-month notice or equal month/duration wages paid in lieu of the notice. The wages in this case will be worked out on the average of the last three month's pay drawn. For the contract or temporary employees, the termination notice period may vary depending on the employment or project duration. An employee will be only terminated by a prior 15 days’ notice if the project (original or remaining)/employment duration is minimum 3 months and 3–7 days in case the project/employment duration is minimum 10 days, otherwise NIDA-Pak will be liable to pay sum equal to the termination period mentioned in the employment contract/agreement.

When, on the abolition/reduction of posts in a cadre, the services of an employee are required to be terminated, the employee shall be the junior most in that cadre. Services of an employee may be dispensed with in the event of budgetary constraints, or abolition of a particular post. Such cases shall be dealt with in accordance with seniority, efficiency and performance.
