Management Rights

Management rights under the Statute are described in 5 U.S.C. 7106 (a). These include the right to:

  • Determine the agency’s mission, budget, organization, number of employees, and internal security practices.
  • Hire, assign, direct, lay off, retain, suspend, remove, reduce in grade or pay, or discipline employees.
  • Assign work, make determinations about contracting out and personnel to conduct operations, and select and appoint employees.
  • Take whatever action to carry out Agency’s mission during emergencies.

The Statute requires many of these management rights to be exercised in accordance with applicable laws. The FLRA has broadly defined applicable law to include other Federal statutes and governmentwide regulations. It is important to note that the Statute contains three exceptions to these management rights. These are described on the next screen.
