Financial Management Records and Reporting

The following key financial topics are outlined with expandable sections to provide detailed insights into financial record-keeping, reporting, and reconciliation mechanisms. Click on the + and - icons to expand or hide each section.

+ Financial Books
  • Bank Book (Monthly): Tracks all bank transactions, deposits, withdrawals, and balances on a monthly basis to maintain accurate financial records and ensure transparency.
  • Cash Book (Bi-Monthly): Records all cash receipts and payments, updated bi-monthly, ensuring proper tracking of petty cash and operational expenses.
  • Cash Count: Surprise or scheduled cash count conducted weekly or monthly by supervisors to ensure alignment between physical cash and financial records.
  • Bank Reconciliation (Monthly): A systematic comparison of bank statements with the bank book to identify discrepancies, pending transactions, or financial anomalies.
+ Book Keeping
  • Posting of Daily Expense in QuickBooks: All expenses are recorded daily in the QuickBooks accounting system to ensure real-time financial tracking.
  • Daily Financial Closure: End-of-day reconciliation of all cash, bank, and ledger transactions to maintain accurate financial status.
  • Monthly Financial Closure: Compilation and verification of all financial records for the month, including ledgers, vouchers, and reports, to close financial books.
  • Extraction of Monthly Reports (Hard Copy): Monthly financial summaries and ledgers are printed in hard copy format for filing and audit purposes.
+ Fund Request and Transfer
  • Financial Report / Fund Flow Preparation: Detailed financial report prepared to request funds, presenting budget utilization and projected financial needs.
  • Narrative Report: Explanatory report outlining financial utilization, progress, challenges, and compliance with donor requirements.
  • Bank Reconciliation: Reconciliation report attached with fund transfer request to confirm accurate balances.
  • Verification of Original Documents: Supporting documents verified before processing fund requests or transfers, ensuring authenticity and compliance.
  • Bank Account Details: Account information shared to facilitate fund transfer, including IBAN, account name, and bank branch.
  • Fund Transfer as per Contract: Funds transferred according to contract terms, ensuring compliance with donor agreements and disbursement schedules.
+ Financial Report
  • Financial Report (Donor Template): Reports prepared using donor-approved formats, highlighting budget use, expenditures, commitments, and variances.
  • General Ledger: Comprehensive record of all financial transactions, categorized and posted according to accounting codes.
  • Trial Balance: Summary of all ledger balances to verify accuracy and ensure that debits equal credits.
  • Income and Expenditure Statement: Statement showing revenues, grants, and expenditures to determine financial performance over a specific period.
  • Bank Account Details: Bank account information included to validate balances, reconciliations, and fund status.
