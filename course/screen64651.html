Union Rights

Union rights and duties under the Statute are articulated in 5 U.S.C. 7114. The rights of an exclusive representative include the rights to:

  • Serve as  the exclusive representative of employees in the bargaining unit and act for and negotiate collective bargaining agreements for all employees in the bargaining unit
  • Be represented at formal discussions, or investigatory interviews upon an employee’s request
  • Receive advance notice of any proposed changes to established conditions of employment and the opportunity to negotiate over these changes
  • Request and receive "data" under certain circumstances

It is important for ER specialists to be aware of the legal requirements related to a "formal discussion." In this context "discussion" is synonymous with meeting. If a meeting or discussion with bargaining unit employees meets the criteria of the Statute — i.e., is between one or more representatives of the agency and one or more employees in the unit or their union representatives, and it concerns any grievance or personnel practices or other general conditions of employment — and has the characteristics of being formal (which is discussed in detail later in this module), then the union must be formally invited to the meeting, and the union must be allowed to actively participate in the meeting. However, the union is not permitted to be disruptive of the meeting. If the representatives are disruptive, they can be asked to leave.
