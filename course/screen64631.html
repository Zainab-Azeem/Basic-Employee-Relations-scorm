Summary

This lesson explains how logistics and procurement support humanitarian and operational activities by ensuring that the right goods reach the right place, at the right time, in the right quantity, quality, and price. Logistics begins with a detailed assessment, gathering information on transport, warehouse capacity, availability of goods, pricing, regulations, and local partners. This assessment guides operational planning and helps determine feasibility based on cost, geography, and availability. Logistics works closely with program teams to understand objectives, number of beneficiaries, required items, and delivery locations. Based on this, logistics prepares a Plan of Action, identifying required staff, warehouses, transport needs, mobilisation routes, and distribution mechanisms. Procurement is based on principles of best value for money, fair competition, transparency, non-discrimination, integrity, and fraud control. Clear procedures are necessary to protect organizational interests and comply with donor regulations.
