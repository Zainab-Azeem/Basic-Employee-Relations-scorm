What Changes Need to be Negotiated with the Union? (continued)

On the other hand, not all conditions of employment are negotiable. For example, matters involving political activities (as defined by the Hatch Act), classification of positions, and those matters specifically provided for by Federal statute, are excluded from the definition of conditions of employment that is shown on the next screen. 

There are also exceptions to the general requirement that an agency provide advance notice and an opportunity to bargain prior to making changes to conditions of employment for bargaining unit employees. For example, the agency and union have already negotiated and reached agreement on the topic, or when the union has waived its right to bargain. Labor Relations Specialists should always be consulted when an agency is assessing whether it has an obligation to bargain over any topic.
