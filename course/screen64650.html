Employee Rights

Federal employee rights under the Statute are set out in 5 U.S.C. §7102. Some of the key legal rights it confers on individual employees include the rights to:

  • Form, join, or assist a union
  • Not form, join, or assist a union
  • Act as a representative for a union
  • Bargain collectively through a union with respect to conditions of employment
  • Exercise these rights without fear of reprisal.

To interfere with these statutory rights is a violation of the Statute. Agency representatives should never engage in a reprisal against an employee because they have exercised these rights. The agency could be subject to the filing of an unfair labor practice charge. If the agency is found to have committed an unfair labor practice, it will be required to remedy the violation, which may include, for example, paying money to affected individuals to restore lost pay and will normally be required to post a notice to employees, stating that it will cease and desist the unlawful conduct. This can cause embarrassment to the agency and erode management’s trust and confidence in the advice of the ER specialist.
