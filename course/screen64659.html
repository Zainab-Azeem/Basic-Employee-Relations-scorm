Definition of Management Official

The definition of a "management official" may be found at 5 U.S.C. 7103(a)(11). In short, a management official is an individual who formulates, determines, or influences the policies of an agency.

The definition has been interpreted and clarified by case law; see 7 FLRA 172 which is available at www.flra.gov.

As with supervisors, the Statute and case law define what a "management official" is under the Statute, and this definition may differ from the agency’s classification of a position as “managerial.” Under the definition in the Statute, "management officials" formulate, determine, and influence agency policy. Some managers, for example, as a part of their job duties, do so with respect to agency labor relations. They may, for example, serve as chief spokesman on the agency bargaining team and have authority to reach agreement on conditions of employment without checking with anyone. It is important to note that the subject matter experts for a given work function within an agency may or may not be a Management official.
