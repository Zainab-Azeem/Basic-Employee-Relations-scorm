Grievances

A grievance is defined in 5 U.S.C. 7103(a)(9) as any complaint filed by a union or employee about:

  • Matters relating to the employment of the employee.
  • Interpretation or claim of breach of a CBA.
  • Violation, misinterpretation, or misapplication of any law, rule, or regulation affecting employment.

Every CBA must have a grievance procedure ending in binding arbitration.

Arbitration is a method of resolving employment disputes through recourse to an impartial third party whose decision is usually final and binding.
