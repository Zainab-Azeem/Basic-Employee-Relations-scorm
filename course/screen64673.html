The right to a union representative at an investigatory interview is often referred to as the "Weingarten Right." This is named for a Supreme Court decision concerning similar rights for private sector employees. A Union must be given the opportunity to be represented at an examination of a unit employee by an agency representative in connection with an investigation if:

  • The employee reasonably believes the examination may result in disciplinary action (but "reasonable belief" is not intended to be subjective to the employee; rather, it would be based on if a reasonable person, in light of the external evidence, would believe that discipline may result). 
  • The employee requests union representation.
There are really five elements that must be satisfied to trigger Weingarten rights: 
  1. An examination of a bargaining unit employee 
  2. By an agency representative
  3. In connection with an investigation 
  4. Reasonable belief of the bargaining unit employee that the examination may result in disciplinary action
  5. The employee requests union representation
